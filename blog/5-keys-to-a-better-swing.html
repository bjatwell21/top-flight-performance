5 Keys to a Better Swing

I've worked with hitters at every level — from middle school kids just learning the game to guys with MLB experience. And I can tell you this: the best hitters in the world aren't doing anything that's complicated. They're just doing the simple things exceptionally well.

Here are five things I go back to, every time, with every hitter I work with.

1. Your Setup Determines Everything

Before the ball is even pitched, your swing is already being shaped by where you stand, how your feet are positioned, and how your hands sit. A poor setup creates problems you then have to try to compensate for mid-swing — and that's a losing battle. When I work with hitters, we spend serious time just getting the setup right. It sounds boring, but it matters more than anything that comes after it.

2. Rotational Power Comes from the Ground Up

Every ounce of power in your swing starts at your feet. Your ability to drive into the ground, rotate through your hips, and transfer that energy into your hands is the difference between a routine ground ball and a ball that jumps off the bat. I see this constantly when I work with guys who've been swinging the same way for years — once we fix the lower half, everything else starts to click.

3. The Barrel Has to Work on Plane

One of the most common issues I see, especially at the high school level, is hitters who are technically doing a lot of things right, but whose barrel is cutting across or under the hitting zone instead of staying on plane with the pitch. When you get the barrel working on plane early and keep it there through contact, your margin for error goes way up. You start hitting pitches you'd normally miss.

4. Stop Thinking About Hitting During Your At-Bat

This one surprises people. The time for mechanical thoughts is in the cage, in practice. At the plate, your job is to see the ball and react. When I coached at Hesston College and MidAmerica Nazarene, one of the things we emphasized constantly was the mental side — the ability to trust your work and get out of your own way. The hitters who broke records, who went deep in the postseason, weren't the ones thinking about their hands at the plate. They were locked in on the pitch.

5. Consistency Beats Brilliance

The hitters I've seen develop the fastest are the ones who show up, do the work, and stay consistent — not the ones who have one great session and disappear for two weeks. The program we run at TFP is built around that principle. Structured, intentional work, every session, reinforced through the TrainHeroic app between sessions. That's how real development happens.

If you're ready to build your swing the right way — from the ground up, with a system behind it — come in for an evaluation. We'll identify exactly where you are and build a plan to get you where you want to be.
