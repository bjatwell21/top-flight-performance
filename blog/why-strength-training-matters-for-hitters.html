Why Strength Training Matters for Hitters

When I was coming up as a player at the University of Central Missouri and later in pro ball with the Wichita Wingnuts, I thought hitting was about technique. Get the mechanics right, put in the reps, and results would follow. And that's true — to a point.

What I learned over time, and what I've since studied deeply through my Master's degree in Exercise Science and my CSCS certification, is that you cannot separate skill from athleticism. You just can't.

Exit Velocity Is Physical

The hardest-hit balls come from the most explosive athletes. That's not an opinion — it's physics. Force equals mass times acceleration. The stronger and more explosive you are, the more force you can generate at contact. We can fine-tune a swing all day, but if the athlete doesn't have the physical capacity to back it up, we're working against a ceiling.

Power Is Trained, Not Given

A lot of parents and athletes believe that strength and explosiveness are just genetic — you either have it or you don't. That's not how it works. Power is a trainable quality. When we combine hitting instruction with structured strength programming at TFP, we're building the physical foundation that allows a hitter's mechanics to actually translate into production. Athletes who commit to the full program — cage work and strength work together — develop significantly faster than those who only do one or the other.

Durability Matters as Much as Performance

One of the most overlooked benefits of strength training for hitters is injury prevention. A stronger athlete is a more resilient athlete. The demands of a long season — especially at the high school and college level — break down bodies that aren't prepared. We build our strength programs to address the specific demands of rotational athletes, protecting the joints and muscles that take the most stress over the course of a season. The best ability is availability, and that starts with building a body that holds up.

The TrainHeroic Connection

Every TFP athlete gets a complete training program through the TrainHeroic app. That means the strength work doesn't live and die in one session with us — it's something athletes can do on their own, structured around exactly what we're working on together in the cage. It's the connective tissue between sessions that makes the difference. When an athlete shows up for a lesson having put in the physical work all week, everything moves faster.

I didn't build TFP to be a hitting facility that also has some weights in the corner. I built it to develop complete athletes — because that's what gets players to the next level.
