The Problem with Random Lessons

I've been coaching hitters at one level or another for a while now — junior college, NAIA, and now privately through TFP. And one of the most consistent things I see when athletes come to us is the same problem: they've been taking lessons, but there's no system behind it.

They show up once a week, work on something different every time, go home, and don't touch a bat until next week. Then they wonder why they're not improving.

That's not a criticism of anyone — it's just the reality of how most hitting instruction is set up. And I get it. Most facilities aren't designed to do anything more than that. They sell lesson packs, put you in a cage, and that's the product.

Random lessons don't create elite hitters. Systems do.

What I Saw at the Highest Level

When I coached at MidAmerica Nazarene in 2023 — the year our team went to the World Series and won our first-ever game there — the hitters who performed in the biggest moments weren't the most naturally talented guys. They were the most prepared. They had a system. They knew exactly what they were working on, why they were working on it, and what it was supposed to feel like come game time.

That clarity doesn't happen by accident. It comes from intentional, structured development that builds on itself week after week. When every session connects to the last one, and athletes know what they're chasing, the work actually sticks.

The Issue with One-Off Lessons

Here's what typically happens with the random lesson model. Week one, an instructor works on a hitter's load. Week two, a different thought about hip rotation. Week three, something about the hands. There's no thread connecting any of it. The hitter goes away confused, their body never gets to rep anything enough to make it automatic, and their feel for the swing is a patchwork of half-implemented ideas from different sessions.

Contrast that with a structured program where session two builds directly on session one, where the between-session work reinforces what was covered in the cage, and where the hitter comes back next week having already internalized what they left with. That's how real development works.

What We Do Differently at TFP

Every athlete at TFP gets a structured program. Every session builds on the last. The TrainHeroic app keeps development going between sessions so nothing gets lost. And the 2-on-1 format — two athletes, one coach — means that when you're in the cage, every single rep has a purpose. Nobody's standing around. Nobody's going through the motions.

We also start every new athlete with an evaluation. Not to upsell anything, but because we genuinely need to know where you are before we can build a plan to get you where you want to be. The plan matters. Without it, you're just hitting balls.

If you're taking lessons somewhere and you can't tell me what you're working on or why — that's a problem worth solving. Come talk to us.
